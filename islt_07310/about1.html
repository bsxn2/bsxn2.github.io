Educator Credentials

My name is Brianna Shipp and I have worked as an educator in a public school district for seven years. I am a mother of two daughters. I have a Master's degree in Educational Technology from Lindenwood University. I attended Harris Stowe State University, where I earned my Bachelor's in Education with an emphasis in Social Sciences. Currently, I am a member of the School of Information Science and Learning Technologies and working on my Ed. S degree in Learning Technology from the University of Missouri. I am certified to teach grades 5-9 and have worked with middle school students in grades six, seven, and eight. Throughout my career, I have developed a passion for presenting my students with the opportunity to enhance their learning using technology. Recently, I have developed the desire to aid fellow educators in expanding their pedagogy to include strategies and tools centered around technology incorporation in the classroom. Course Description
EDU52000 - Curriculum Analysis and Design Software Engineering covers the principles, processes, and professional practices used to design, develop, test, deploy, and manage software systems in a team-based, collaborative environment. A range of software engineering methodologies are covered with an emphasis on agile software development using incremental methods of managing the development activities.
EDT50030 - Multimedia Applications This course will enable participants to use digital communication tools to effectively communicate in different media, be it websites, video, audio, text, or animated multimedia. Participants will select the right medium for the message and create different forms of communication for students, parents and peers. Course participants will be able to describe digital literacy, what it is, and its importance as a skill for this generation of learners. Topics and software such as podcast, discussion boards, websites, video, information literacy, acceptable use, privacy and security policies, digital etiquette and responsible social interactions will be covered.
EDT50020 - Transforming Learning with Technology This course is designed to enhance and extend the technology skills of practicing individuals, apply those skills in innovative ways, and create an environment that supports collaborative, project-based learning. Participants will study basic and advanced features of software (operating system, word processing, spreadsheets, presentation software, Internet, concept mapping) and hardware (interactive white boards, projectors, wireless technology, projectors, cameras) and integrating technology.
EDT51900 - Leadership in Education In this course, students will explore various theories and models of leadership and the unique challenges associated with leading in an educational setting. By means of group and individual exercises, students will examine their own skill sets and personality traits in order to develop a personal philosophy of leadership.
EDT50900 - Innovation Mindset This course examines the role of innovation in education as a theory and a practice. Students will incorporate new educational technology tools into their learning through projects designed to foster innovative thinking and a growth mindset.
